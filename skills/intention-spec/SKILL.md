# Intention Spec

Turn a fuzzy want into a written spec. This is Napoleon Hill's "definiteness of purpose" in modern terms: a vision with no spec stays a vision, but a single, specific, written outcome engages both your attention filter and a real plan. Specificity is the whole job.

## When to use

The user has a goal that's still vague, says they want to "get clear", or is about to build toward something without having defined what "it" actually is.

## Steps

1. **Extract the outcome.** Ask what they want, then push past the abstraction until it's one concrete, observable result. "Be a writer" → "publish a 10-essay collection people pay for by Q3."
2. **Name who it's for.** Even personal goals have a beneficiary. Who is changed when this is real? This sharpens the outcome and the motivation.
3. **Define done.** Write the exact condition that means finished, plus the quality bar it must clear. "Done" must be something you could point at and verify.
4. **Surface the obstacle.** The single biggest real thing in the way. Naming it (mental contrasting) is what separates a plan from a fantasy.
5. **First move.** The one concrete action that starts it — small enough to do this week.

## Output

```
---
intention: [kebab-case-name]
updated: [YYYY-MM-DD]
---
## Outcome
[one concrete, observable result]

## For
[who is changed when this is real]

## Done when
[the exact finished-condition + quality bar]

## Obstacle
[the single biggest real thing in the way]

## First move
[one action you can take this week]
```

## Hand-off

A completed intention spec is the input to the `reality-architect-loop` skill (it becomes the "Design" artifact) and to the Reality Architect method at [realityarchitect.ai](https://realityarchitect.ai/method).

## Boundaries

- Keep outcomes honest and within the user's agency. "Win the lottery" isn't a spec; "ship the product that could earn" is.
- One intention per spec. If it has two done-conditions, it's two specs.
